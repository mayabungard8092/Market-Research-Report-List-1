<p><ul><li>Mechanical System</li><li>Hydraulic System</li><li>Electric System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Automotive Traction Control System (TCS) market includes three primary types: Mechanical, Hydraulic, and Electric Systems. Mechanical TCS relies on physical components like differentials to manage wheel slip, offering simplicity but limited precision. Hydraulic TCS uses fluid pressure to modulate braking force, providing better control and responsiveness. Electric TCS, the most advanced, employs sensors and electronic control units to optimize traction dynamically, ensuring superior performance, efficiency, and integration with modern vehicle systems like ABS and ESC. Each system caters to varying vehicle needs and technological advancements.</p></p>
