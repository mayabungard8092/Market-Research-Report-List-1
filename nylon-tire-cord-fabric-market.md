<p><p>The Nylon Tire Cord Fabric market includes various types based on denier specifications, which indicate the thickness and strength of the yarn. Common types are 840/2, 1260/2, 1260/3, 1680/2, 1890/2, 1890/3, and 2520/2 denier. The first number represents the yarn's thickness, while the second denotes the number of plies twisted together. Higher denier values indicate thicker, stronger cords, suitable for heavy-duty tires, while lower denier types are used for lighter applications. These fabrics enhance tire durability, flexibility, and performance across different vehicle types.</p></p>
