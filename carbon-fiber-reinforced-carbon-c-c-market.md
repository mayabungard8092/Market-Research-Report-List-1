<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Liquid Impregnation Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Carbon Fiber-Reinforced Carbon (C & C) market is primarily segmented based on production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. CVD involves depositing a carbon-rich gas onto a substrate, creating a dense, high-purity layer of carbon fibers, ideal for high-performance applications like aerospace and defense. In contrast, the Liquid Impregnation Process involves saturating carbon fibers with a liquid resin and curing it, resulting in a composite with improved mechanical properties, suitable for various industrial applications.</p></p>
