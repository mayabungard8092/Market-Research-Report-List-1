<p><p>The Plant Source Hydrocolloids Market is categorized into several types based on their sources and properties. Cellulose and its derivatives, derived from plant cell walls, serve as thickening agents and stabilizers. Hemicellulose, a component of plant cell walls, offers emulsifying and gelling properties. Pectin, extracted from fruits, is widely used in jellies and jams for its gelling capabilities. Exudate gums, produced by plants as a protective response, act as thickening agents. Other market segments include various lesser-known plant-derived hydrocolloids used in diverse applications.</p></p>
