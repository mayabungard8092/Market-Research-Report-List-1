<p><ul><li>Serial</li><li>TTL</li><li>USB</li><li>Ethernet</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Digital I/O cards serve as interfaces between computers and external devices, enabling digital signals for control and communication. The market includes several types: </p><p>1. **Serial** cards, utilizing protocols like RS-232, are ideal for point-to-point connections.  </p><p>2. **TTL** (Transistor-Transistor Logic) cards provide high-speed data transfer, suitable for low-voltage applications.  </p><p>3. **USB** cards offer convenient plug-and-play functionality, widely adopted for their ease of use.  </p><p>4. **Ethernet** cards support networked communication, enabling devices to connect over local or internet-based networks.</p></p>
