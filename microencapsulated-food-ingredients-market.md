<p><p>The microencapsulated food ingredients market includes various technologies for encapsulating flavors, nutrients, and active compounds. Spray technologies involve atomizing ingredients into tiny droplets, allowing for uniform coating and rapid drying. Emulsion technologies create stable mixtures of oil and water, enhancing ingredient stability and bioavailability. Dripping technologies allow for precise droplet formation, ensuring controlled release qualities. Other methods may include coacervation and freeze-drying, each offering unique benefits for preserving and delivering food ingredients effectively.</p></p>
