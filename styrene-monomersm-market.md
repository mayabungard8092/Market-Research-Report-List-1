<p><ul><li>Ethylbenzene Dehydrogenation Method</li><li>Ethylbenzene Oxidation Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Styrene Monomer (SM) is primarily produced through two main methods: Ethylbenzene Dehydrogenation and Ethylbenzene Oxidation. The Ethylbenzene Dehydrogenation Method involves the partial oxidation of ethylbenzene to produce styrene and hydrogen, offering high yields and efficiency. Conversely, the Ethylbenzene Oxidation Method converts ethylbenzene to styrene through catalytic oxidation, typically resulting in a lower yield compared to dehydrogenation. Both methods play a crucial role in meeting global SM demand, influencing the market dynamics significantly.</p></p>
