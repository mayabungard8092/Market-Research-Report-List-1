<p><p>The high voltage (HV) insulator market encompasses three main types: porcelain insulators, glass insulators, and composite insulators. Porcelain insulators are known for their durability and resistance to weather, making them a traditional choice in power transmission. Glass insulators offer high visibility and excellent electrical properties, but they can be heavier and more fragile. Composite insulators, made from polymer materials, are lightweight and offer superior performance in polluted environments. Each type serves specific applications based on performance, cost, and environmental factors.</p></p>
